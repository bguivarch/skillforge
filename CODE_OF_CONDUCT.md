# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Expected Behavior

- **Be Respectful**: Treat others with respect, dignity, and courtesy
- **Be Inclusive**: Welcome and support all contributors regardless of background
- **Be Collaborative**: Work together constructively and share knowledge openly
- **Be Helpful**: Provide assistance and guidance when others need help
- **Be Professional**: Maintain professional conduct in all interactions
- **Be Considerate**: Consider the impact of your words and actions on others

### Unacceptable Behavior

- **Harassment**: Any form of harassment, including unwelcome comments, advances, or jokes
- **Discrimination**: Discriminatory language, jokes, or actions based on personal characteristics
- **Personal Attacks**: Insults, ad hominem attacks, or personal criticism
- **Trolling**: Deliberate provocation or disruption of discussions
- **Spam**: Unwanted promotional content, off-topic messages, or repetitive posting
- **Privacy Violations**: Sharing private information about others without consent
- **Threats**: Any form of threat, intimidation, or violent language
- **Destructive Behavior**: Actions that harm the community or project

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to all community spaces, including but not limited to:

- GitHub repositories (issues, pull requests, discussions)
- Official communication channels
- Community events and meetups
- Any other official SkillForge spaces

This also applies when an individual is officially representing the project or community in public spaces.

## Enforcement

### Reporting

If you witness or experience conduct that violates this code of conduct, please report it to:

- **Email**: benoitguivarch@gmail.com
- **GitHub**: Create a private issue by contacting any maintainer

All reports will be handled confidentially and investigated promptly.

### Investigation Process

1. **Receipt**: We acknowledge receipt of the report within 24 hours
2. **Investigation**: We investigate the report thoroughly and impartially
3. **Action**: We take appropriate action based on the investigation
4. **Follow-up**: We follow up with the reporter as appropriate

### Consequences

Consequences for violating this code of conduct may include:

- **Warning**: A private warning about the behavior
- **Temporary Suspension**: Temporary removal from community spaces
- **Permanent Ban**: Permanent removal from community spaces
- **Other Actions**: Additional actions as deemed necessary

### Appeals

If you believe you have been unfairly accused or punished, you may appeal by:

1. Contacting a different community maintainer
2. Providing additional context or evidence
3. Requesting a review by the project leadership

## Community Guidelines

### Communication

- **Language**: Use inclusive and welcoming language
- **Tone**: Maintain a constructive and positive tone
- **Feedback**: Provide feedback that is specific, actionable, and kind
- **Disagreements**: Disagree respectfully and focus on ideas, not people

### Contribution Guidelines

- **Quality**: Ensure contributions meet project quality standards
- **Documentation**: Provide clear documentation for new features
- **Testing**: Test your contributions thoroughly
- **Collaboration**: Work with maintainers to improve contributions

### Conflict Resolution

- **Direct Resolution**: Try to resolve conflicts directly and respectfully
- **Mediation**: Request mediation from community maintainers if needed
- **Escalation**: Escalate serious conflicts to project leadership

## Project Leadership

### Maintainer Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- **Clarifying Standards**: Clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior
- **Leading by Example**: Modeling positive community behavior
- **Addressing Issues**: Promptly addressing reported conduct issues
- **Maintaining Safety**: Ensuring community spaces are safe and welcoming

### Leadership Commitment

Project leadership commits to:

- **Transparency**: Being transparent about community decisions and processes
- **Fairness**: Applying this code of conduct fairly and consistently
- **Accessibility**: Making community spaces accessible to all
- **Improvement**: Continuously improving our community practices
